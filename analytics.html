Dashboard Page Explorer

An in-depth breakdown of the visuals, data endpoints, and CRO strategies modeled across each dashboard page from Google's Merchandise Store GA4 stream.

The primary control panel of the dashboard. It delivers a high-level, consolidated diagnostic pulse of total user traffic, engagement rate, transaction velocity, and overall revenue yield at a glance.

  • Visuals & Charts: Dual-metric Traffic Over Time line chart (Sessions vs. Total Revenue), channel distribution pie chart (Direct, Organic, CPC, Referral), landing page table, and a detailed Traffic by User/Pageviews daily bar chart.
  • Data Discovery & Logic: Sums total e-commerce events and processes aggregate metrics including Total Users (89,615), Sessions (105,601), Views (336,327), and Conversion Rate (1.03%), dynamically mapping GTM triggers against GA4 stream parameters.
  • CRO Strategy Playbook: Uncovers critical traffic drops and transactional deviations. Despite solid traffic, the low conversion rate (1.03%) highlights a major checkout abandonment bottleneck that requires immediate form-field optimization.

Demystifies the store's user cohorts by dissecting demographic profiles, device preferences, browser ecosystems, and temporal engagement patterns.

  • Visuals & Charts: Hourly and day-of-week engagement charts, demographic age brackets (dominated by 18-24 and 25-34 groups), gender splits (62.1% Male / 37.9% Female), device category bars, and operating system / browser breakdown tables.
  • Data Discovery & Logic: Extracts demographic and system segments from browser user-agent strings and Google Signals, mapping sessions to variables like device.category, device.operating_system, and device.web_info.browser.
  • CRO Strategy Playbook: Mobile sessions (62,431) massively dominate desktop (44,879), yet Safari browser traffic accounts for only 4,863 users (compared to Chrome's 77,035). This massive gap points to potential Safari rendering bugs or Safari-pay checkout errors that need to be audited.

Exposes the spatial distribution of your traffic, cross-referencing user density with local monetary value to identify geographic zones with the highest return on investment.

  • Visuals & Charts: Donut charts tracking Top Country by User (led by United States at 50.9% and India at 15.1%) and Top Region by User (California leading at 41.7%), paired with an interactive world map and regional ledger.
  • Data Discovery & Logic: Computes Per User Valuation (Total Revenue / Total Users) and Average Purchase Revenue (Total Revenue / Transactions) using IP-to-location geo-lookups.
  • CRO Strategy Playbook: California and Ontario yield premium valuations (e.g., $16.73 per user in San Francisco and $12.73 in Toronto). Conversely, India holds a high user volume (15.1% of total) but yields $0.00 revenue, signaling localized shipping limits or payment gateway integration gaps.

A full-screen, high-precision spatial mapping interface designed to track physical store locations, popup events, or regional warehouse shipping distributions.

  • Visuals & Charts: A highly interactive Google Maps component supporting satellite overlays, embedded with custom pins highlighting high-density transaction coordinates (e.g., Grand Indonesia, Thamrin Nine, and Bundaran HI).
  • Data Discovery & Logic: Dynamically loads precise coordinate values (latitude and longitude arrays) mapped from shipping address inputs or physical point-of-sale (POS) terminal uploads.
  • CRO Strategy Playbook: Allows for hyper-localized offline promotions and geo-fenced search ad campaigns within a 5-mile radius of pins showing high physical sales volume.

The ultimate checkout health-monitor. This screen compares cart additions, checkouts, and transactional yields against the previous year to identify friction leaks in the purchase journey.

  • Visuals & Charts: Five comparative Year-over-Year (YoY) line charts (Add to Carts, Checkouts, Transactions, Shipping, and Revenue) paired with circular, trend-tracking diagnostic indicators.
  • Data Discovery & Logic: Traces e-commerce event successions—from add_to_cart to begin_checkout to purchase—calculating period-over-period percentage variances (e.g., Add to Carts: 11,682, -20%; Revenue: $186,587, -17%).
  • CRO Strategy Playbook: A severe drop-off is visible between Cart Adds (11,682) and Checkouts (3,808). Mitigate this by introducing slide-out cart drawers, mini-cart reminders, or automated recovery triggers to convert cart-adders.

Audits the financial yield of marketing channels, isolating revenue from direct links, paid ads, organic channels, and referring domains to optimize acquisition budgets.

  • Visuals & Charts: YoY channel trend charts, revenue-share donut charts, and a detailed Source / Medium ledger detailing referral contributions.
  • Data Discovery & Logic: Isolates transaction streams using attribution modeling, joining purchase values with traffic_source.source and traffic_source.medium parameters.
  • CRO Strategy Playbook: Direct traffic dominates revenue (51.9%, $96,803), while referral channels display strong positive growth (+10%, $15,285) led by domains like art-analytics.appspot.com ($8,848). Partnerships and cross-promotions with these high-converting referral sources should be prioritized.

Connects search query intent directly with marketing spend. It audits Google Ads keywords to verify which search queries drive e-commerce revenue and which waste budget.

  • Visuals & Charts: Daily Ad Cost vs. Revenue trend charts, a keyword click performance ledger (mapping impressions, clicks, CPC, and ad cost), and a keyword-level revenue yield ledger.
  • Data Discovery & Logic: Integrates Google Ads and GA4 datasets using GCLID parameters, grouping ads performance metrics directly with checkout purchase revenue.
  • CRO Strategy Playbook: Branded search (google merchandise store) drives high clicks (784) at a low CPC ($0.30). However, a massive portion of ads revenue ($19,183 of $20,739) is labeled as (not set), indicating a severe auto-tagging or parameter-tracking bug that needs to be resolved immediately.

A granular audit of product performance. It tracks views, purchase volume, and sales revenue for individual items to isolate catalog performance and high-appeal items.

  • Visuals & Charts: An item ledger showing Items Viewed, % change, Items Purchased, and Item Revenue, accompanied by daily purchase volume dual bars and purchase velocity line graphs.
  • Data Discovery & Logic: Parses GA4 product-scoped e-commerce dimensions within the items array, pulling and aggregating parameters like item_name, quantity, and item_revenue.
  • CRO Strategy Playbook: The Google Regatta Tote has high details views (445) but low transactions, indicating that pricing, photos, or descriptions on the product page are causing friction. Conversely, low-friction add-ons like the Google Bike Party Sticker sell in massive quantities (784 items purchased, $3,136 revenue) with almost zero detail views, indicating they should be promoted as checkout cross-sells.
